Risks

Blood donation is safe. New, sterile disposable equipment is used for each donor
so there's no risk of contracting a bloodborne infection by donating blood.

If you're a healthy adult:
you can usually donate a pint (about half a liter) of blood without endangering your health.
Within a few days of a blood donation, your body replaces the lost fluids.
And after two weeks, your body replaces the lost red blood cells.
